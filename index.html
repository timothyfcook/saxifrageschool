The Saxifrage School is redesigning higher education by lowering costs, re-thinking the campus, and reconciling theory and practice. We teach students to make what is valuable and to question the value of what is made.

Our Programs

Crash Course

Learn to Build, Program, Grow, and discover what you love to do. This 14-day intensive program offers students ages 14+ a diverse and powerful education in productive inquiry.

Build

Spots are available for our Spring, 2013 twelve-week course.

Learn both to Design and Build furniture and small structures. Projects may include the construction of a houseboat, bioshelter, tiny-home, or chicken coop. Study the history, philosophy and economics of residential construction and permaculture carpentry practices.

Program

Spots are available for our Spring, 2013 twelve-week course.

The theory and practice of Web Development for beginners. Join us for a single course, or a full-time, twelve-week boot camp. Topics will include: HTML5, CSS3, Python, Git, UX, Open-Source philosophy, Hacker culture, and human-computer interaction.

Grow

Spots are available for our Spring, 2013 twelve-week course.

The theory and practice of organic agriculture for beginners. Join us for a single course, or a full-time, twelve-week boot camp. Topics will include: composting, enivornmental ethics, soil science, food politics, companion planting, crop rotation, planting, pest/disease control, seed saving, permaculture, and season extension.

Summer Crash Course

Do not merely study: live life earnestly.
More information coming soon.

The Crash Course, coming Summer 2013. A 14-day program for students ages 14 and Up. Students learn the basics of three major skills sets (Carpentry, Programming, and Farming), while thinking critically and creatively about complex problems in real-world contexts. Students will be challenged to reconcile theory with practice, learning not just to critique, but to invent. July 15 - 28

Applications Not Yet Open

Day 1: The Basics

A one-day orientation to your tools, the group, and our learning philosophy. Start seeds to grow.

Day 2: HTML

Learn to code. Make a basic website. Discuss hackerdom.

Day 3 & 4: CSS/JS

Learn to code. Make your website beautiful and interactive. Discuss open-source philosophies.

Days 5-6: Survival

Go on a wilderness survival expedition. Write a story about the next year of your life.

Days 7-9: Building

Learn basic shop skills, safety, and build a small structure. Discuss the history of dwellings, craftsmanship and design.

Day 10: Reflection

Explore the City. Take a day to think, write, and wonder.

Days 11-13: The Farm

Plant seedlings, prepare rows, harvest vegetables, plan the next season. Discuss the ethics, economics, and environmental issues of food production.

Day 14: Conclusion

Finishing touches on your work, present your final products. Look back over the past 14 days; Feast.

The Team

Timothy Cook

Founding Director

Tim has been researching new directions in higher education for the past 5 years. His interests include urban agriculture, carpentry, web development, poetry, and cycling. He is charged with directing the work and vision of the Saxifrage School.

Andrew Heffner

Director of Campus Partnerships

Andrew is the communications expert at the Saxifrage School. Aside from studying Higher Education at the graduate level, Andrew is an serious student of culture: music, film, art, and literature. He is charged with building new partnerships, and taking care of current ones.

Alexa Belajac

Volunteer, New Projects

Alexa is intensely creatively and serious about excellence. She thinks up new ways to move our project forward. She is a mother of three girls.

Justin Seaman

Assistant to the Director

Justin gets it done. More than you could ever know.

Bryan Perry

President, Board of Directors

Bryan has worked for over a decade as a leader in the non-profit world, bringing fundraising and organizational leadership experience to our team. He currently works as an Assistant Director for Oakland Planning and Development Corporation.

Abigail Loughrey

Vice-President, Board of Directors

Abigail studied Social and Comparative Analysis in Education at the University of Pittsburgh and most recently worked for Pittsburgh Public Schools on their Gates Foundation funded initiative for teaching excellence.

Jess Wilton

Board Member, Academic Planning

Jess is completing a PhD program in Literary and Cultural Studies at Carnegie Mellon University.

Chad Whitacre

Programming Instructor

Chad Whitacre is a programmer and entrepreneur with 12 years of professional experience. An active member of the open source community, Chad is the author of the Aspen web framework, as well as tools for mapping and database management. He is also the founder of Gittip.com, a gift economy . Chad holds a B.A. in Ancient Languages from Wheaton College (IL), and lives with his wife and four children in Ambridge, PA. His personal website is www.whit537.org.

Courtney Williams

Agriculture Instructor

Courtney has worked on a variety of organic farms, both rural and urban. Most recently she was the lead educator and grower at the Pittsburgh Community Farm on the City’s north side. She comes from a family of Kentucky farmers and agricultural tool inventors. Her experience also includes significant practice in homesteading, work as a Doula, and studies in anthropology, agricultural history, and nutrition.
