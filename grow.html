Organic Agriculture: Philosophy and Practice

About the Class

Beginning this Spring students will learn about the history, philosophy and practices of organic agriculture. Through lectures and hands-on practice, this intensive course will immerse students in the basic skills and knowledge needed to design, plan, and implement a small scale organic farm. The classroom will be a 1.5 acre urban farm and practical topics will range from composting methods, soil preparation and preservation, bed preparation, companion planting, crop rotation, and planting, to pest and disease control, seed saving, and season extension. Books by Eliot Coleman and John Jeavons will serve as useful farming guides while students will read and discuss other foundational works by Sir Albert Howard and Wendell Berry to gain a greater understanding of the theories and goals of the organic agriculture movement.

Details: $300 Tuition* for 12, three-hour classes; 12 meals included in the cost. Class registration is limited to 14 people. The class will begin the last week of February and run until the beginning of May. *Plus $50 administrative fee.
